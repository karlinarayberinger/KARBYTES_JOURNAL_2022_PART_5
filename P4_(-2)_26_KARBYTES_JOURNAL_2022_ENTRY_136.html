Today I asked myself, “Why don’t you work on calculus problems outdoors yet you draw and update your websites outdoors?”

I believe that I should be just as eager to work on calculus problems outdoors as I am on drawing abstract pictures, yet I have been a lot more inclined to draw rather than to study mathematics like I know I ought to in order to be as smart as I want to be.

I did not want to generate a bunch of answers explaining how I might feel more inclined to draw for social reinforcement reasons and such because that sounds like making excuses to remain in “the pink bin” instead of in “the green bin”.

I will say that I am self conscious and feel that I have procrastinated severely on graduating from college with a bachelor’s degree in computer science and, even worse, I have procrastinated on studying physics and other important subjects on my own when ample online resources are available to make it possible for me to get a college-level education on my own. I used to say that I would prefer to have the structure and the environment of a brick and mortar school, but to be honest, I feel too antisocial and impatient to move on with my life and to put my “college years” behind me. (That does not mean I do not want to advance my education level. It just means that I am tired of being socialized as someone in my late teens or early twenties rather than as someone who is exactly 32 years of age).

Even though I feel self conscious about other people watching me and of other people teasing me and of other people even going so far as to steal my supplies or to block my Internet access, I strongly believe that, if I care enough about doing something, I will be very hard to prevent from doing whatever that thing is. I just have not been as willing to “just do it” as I otherwise could have been (or maybe I really was too mentally fragile to muster up that focus and immunity to being heckled by other people).

To be clear, “the pink bin” is about being too much of a sycophant, people pleaser, and quite frankly, hostage to do much other than stay in the shallow end of the metaphorical swimming pool which represents my mind. Meanwhile, “the green bin” is about being very impersonal, diligent about completing tasks, humbly hard working, and intrinsically motivated to explore ever deeper depths of that metaphorical swimming pool.

What makes transitioning from “the pink bin” to “the green bin” feel like torture for me is the way I am frequently verbally harassed by thought police who force me to listen to demoralizing, distracting, and ad hominem messages to the point that I cannot help but conclude that the thought police do not seriously want me to make that transition.

Why would anyone (particularly the thought police) want to prevent me from making that transition? Perhaps too many people do not want me to feel consistently good about myself. Perhaps too many people are comforted by the prospect of me being held back for their sadistic amusement and so that they have someone to use as a scapegoat to point to as I walk by and they tell their kids that what I am is a social reject and someone forced to be psychologically tortured for not graduating school on time and being more socially conformist.

“She’s not ready to graduate high school,” is what I have heard some soccer moms say in my midst. I do not think they want me to, quite frankly. I resent having to burn myself out just hiking to locations where I can use my laptop where I am not harassed by people, insects, or bad weather conditions.

If those harassing voices would just stop, I think that I would calm down enough to study just about anywhere. But those voices are not getting any kinder nor easier to put up with as time goes by. What is stressing me out more than anything is realizing deep down that having to listen to these voices is literally handicapping my cognitive abilities, diminishing my health, and preventing me from enjoying the hobbies I live for.

I am surprised no one has seriously shown compassion for me even though I have published these kinds of journals many times online. It really does seem that no one besides myself seriously wants me to be happy and to succeed at my goals.

I have to ask, “Why do so many people seem to want me to fail at my goals and to remain very unhappy?” Perhaps plenty of people think I have not been punished enough for crimes I committed in the past. Perhaps plenty of people think I have had too easy of a life for too long and want to “even the score” by forcing me to go through brutal hardships.

Depriving me of happiness does not make the world a happier place. Actually, perhaps it does if multiple people’s happiness levels go up as a consequence of my happiness level going down. I think that is literally how most people attempt to feel good about themselves: by comparing themselves to others and rejoicing in the misfortunes of those they would otherwise be envious of.

If what I say is true, then I really do have to fight tooth and nail just to get a modicum of satisfaction and to enjoy even the most tepid fruits of my own hard labor. As pessimistic as this sounds, I find it useful to assume that people generally would rather see me suffer than see me well. That helps me keep my resolve to remain friendless and to ask as little of others as possible (knowing that they are likely going to disappoint and betray me).

“How am I going to get a paid job if I do not pretend to already have friends I am on good terms with?” I expect that I will need to spend at least three more months training myself to pretend to be in a perpetually okay (and not great) mood and to get used to expressing only politically correct content before I can get real value out of job searching. I am afraid that I will not be able to do one or both of those things. If I cannot do both of those things, then I am basically “too mentally ill” to work for other people. I can only work if I do not mind having my paychecks withheld indefinitely, harassed by customers and coworkers, and having no time for a life outside of work. Perhaps I will be most successful long term if I procrastinate on getting a job until after I turn 33 on 13_JANUARY_2023.

What I plan to spend the rest of the year doing while unemployed is working on my website and practice surviving with as little social support as possible.
