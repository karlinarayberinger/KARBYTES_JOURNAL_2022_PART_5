KARBYTES_JOURNAL_2022_ENTRY_113

While I was in the middle of updating this website and enjoying listening to some of my favorite music, the house Wi-Fi I was using suddenly stopped working. That Wi-Fi stopped working as soon as I noticed that my dad and Steve were home. I walked up the street to use my mobile phone as a point of contact with the Internet to finish this update and I saw a chubby Latina jog by doing some kind of victory dance. On my way out of the bathroom I was hiding in, I thought I heard Steve call me a narcissist. Though I could be misinterpreting what I am perceiving as animosity towards me in particular, I cannot help but suspect that many people are against me doing my art and having a good time. I think they want to punish me for being self absorbed and happy by forcing me to live on the streets without a roof over my head and without privacy and the ability to stream large amounts of data. After this update, I plan on taking a THC pill and playing the drums while I charge this laptop. Then I plan on hiking to some secluded place with decent Internet access to continue working on my laptop. It’s like it is taboo for me to have hobbies at all. It’s like I am not even allowed to have a laptop. It’s like other people would prefer that I feel perpetually terrorized, tortured, and deprived of liberties which others seem to enjoy. It’s like other people are not done punishing me and making me feel like a prisoner. What a rough way to move on from adversity. Even moving on from adversity is treated like a luxury I am not allowed to enjoy. It’s like the people I live with are hellbent on making me settle for less and less with every passing day until I am doing little more than writhing in a corner and letting my physical and mental fitness atrophy so that I am too weak to do anything but settle for being in permanently destitute circumstances.

I do not mind being labeled a narcissist. Deep down, I believe everyone is a narcissist. I do not believe altruism exists as anything more than extended selfishness. Unlike others who make a show of how much they sacrifice themselves to look like devoted servants to other people and to not show off their talents and good fortune, I think that such false humility is pathetic and ugly and makes the world a shittier place. It’s a no-win situation. No matter what I do, it is never enough to please all the people and to permanently end the problems which seem to ruin people’s lives. I thought that simply focusing on my own business was good enough. As I write this, some lady walked past me saying some bitchy words at me. I noticed that I tend to mind my own business and do my best not to encroach on other people’s freedom and privacy while they do not return the favor. I feel that I live amongst humans who are lukewarm tolerant of my existence at best and intrusive and misery mongering more often than not.

What I think is going on is that I am not making a big enough display of my vulnerability, misery, sense of helplessness, and neediness for other people’s attention, approval, and assistance. I have been trying to avoid dwelling unnecessarily long on tragic topics only to find that people are not ready for me to move on with my life. It’s like people secretly do not want the problems which plague humanity to end because they are afraid that humanity has so much more to do than act like helpless victims of circumstance and addicts to sensationalist drama mongering.

I feel that most humans only utilize less than 5% of their genius potential because to utilize more actually takes effort and invites real novelty and real challenge. I think people are addicted to making everyone play small and recycling the same complaints over and over again because that familiarity feels safer than venturing into the unknown and going it alone.

There is seemingly no greater sin than being voluntarily alone; to be one’s own best friend.
